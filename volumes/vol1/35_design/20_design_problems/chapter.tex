\chaptersecond{Monotone Co-Design Theory}{chapter-swissarmyknife}{chap:design-problems}{

    This chapter introduces \emph{Monotone Co-Design Theory}, a formalization for computational design theory.
    It is a compositional theory of which the primitive elements are \emph{design problems} (DPI),
    formalized as relations among functionality, resources, and implementations.

    We show that DPIs can capture \SY{design problems} across diverse fields.

}
