\chaptersecond{Traced Monoidal categories}{chapter-placeholder}{chap:generalization}{
    This chapter describes the classical notions of \SY{monoidal categories} and \SY{traced monoidal categories}.
    In our exposition in~\cref{chap:parallelism}, we assumed that the categories were \SY{associative stacking}.
    That is, $\mora\mtimes(\morb\mtimes\morc)=(\mora\mtimes\morb)\mtimes\morc$.
    One can construct the notion of parallelism with less strict conditions, by asking only that the two results are related by an isomorphism: $\mora\mtimes(\morb\mtimes\morc)\simeq(\mora\mtimes\morb)\mtimes\morc$.
    This requires bookkeeping of \SY{natural transformations} to describe the needed isomorphisms.
}
